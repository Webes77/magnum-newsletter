01 — The Newsline

The most powerful AI model in the world launched on Monday and was switched off by Thursday.

Anthropic released Claude Fable 5, the most capable model anyone had ever been able to use. Three days later it was pulled worldwide after the US government stepped in over safety concerns, the first time a government has ever ordered an AI model off the market. Dramatic, yes. But here’s the part that actually matters to you: while everyone races to the newest, most powerful model, that’s not where your results come from. The tools you already have, used properly, will do more for your business this year than any launch. You’re not behind for ignoring the noise. You’re ahead for it.

02 — Looking Sideways

The best AI users I know have stopped typing

ChatGPT announced its biggest overhaul since launch this week. Agents, app integrations, the works. Everyone’s writing about it. The thing I actually noticed was much smaller. A bloke I know who’s in the car four hours a day, in and out of client sites, runs his entire follow-up list by talking to his phone between stops. He doesn’t type a word. By the time he’s back at the office, the emails are written and the notes are filed.

Here’s the part everyone misses. We’ve all been taught that AI is a screen-and-keyboard thing. Open the app, think of the clever prompt, type it out neatly. That’s exactly why busy operators bounce off it. You don’t have a spare half hour to sit and write. You’ve got four minutes at a red light. The people getting results aren’t better at AI. They just stopped treating it like homework and started treating it like a phone call.

So this week I went looking for what actually changes once you stop typing. Here’s one that stuck with me.

03 — The Win

A solo accountant turned the monthly report grind into 25 minutes.

He runs a one-person accounting practice and dreaded the same job every month: writing plain-English summaries of the numbers for 14 clients who don’t read spreadsheets. He built a prompt that takes each client’s figures and explains what changed and what it means, in language the client actually understands. The job went from about three hours to 25 minutes. The surprise wasn’t the time saved. Clients started replying to say it was the first time they’d actually understood their own accounts.

Take from it: AI isn’t just for doing the work faster. The bigger win is the translation layer between what you know and what your client needs to hear.

05 — Prompt of the Week

From end-of-day voice note to tomorrow’s action list

Setup: This is the prompt that turns a rambling end-of-day voice note into a clean action list and the emails to match. Run it exactly as written first to see what it does, then swap in your own braindump.

Prompt:

You are my operations assistant. I run a commercial furniture business. I'm going to give you a messy voice note I recorded at the end of the day, full of half-finished thoughts. Read it, work out what actually needs doing, and turn it into something I can act on in the next ten minutes. Here's the voice note: "Right, so the Henderson fit-out, the chairs landed but two of the desks turned up damaged, need to call the supplier about replacements, also Henderson's office manager emailed about timing for the install, I need to get back to her, probably Thursday. The cafe job, Marco still hasn't paid the deposit invoice, that's been two weeks now, chase that. The dental clinic reception quote, I told them I'd have it across today and I haven't. Oh and we're low on the grey mesh chairs, only four left, should reorder before they sell out." Give me back three things. One, a prioritised action list, most urgent first, with who I'm contacting next to each item. Two, a short draft email to the office manager confirming the Thursday install. Three, a short draft email chasing Marco's overdue deposit, firm but friendly. Keep the emails plain and human, the way a real person writes, no corporate padding.

Use it for: Record your own end-of-day voice note tomorrow, paste the transcript in place of the example, and you’ll have your morning sorted before you’ve finished your coffee.
