The Newsline

AI moved into Slack this week, and it tells you where this is going.

Anthropic, the company behind Claude, just put its AI straight inside Slack, the chat app a lot of teams already run on. You type @Claude in a channel and it joins in like another teammate, reads what’s been said, and takes a job off your plate. It’s aimed at bigger companies for now, so most of us can’t use this exact version yet, but the direction is the bit worth clocking: AI is moving out of a separate website and into the tools you already work in, your email, your calendar, your messages. When it shows up in something you use, that’s your cue to try it, because the awkward part, going somewhere new to do it, just disappeared.

The Win

A garden centre turned its most annoying question into sales.

A suburban garden centre we know was drowning in one question all day: why is my plant dying, and what goes in this spot. Fair question, but it ate their staff alive, the same answers forty times a day. So they loaded their own plant-care guides and stock list into a simple AI chatbot and stuck it on an iPad at the counter, with a QR code out on the benches. Customers get a straight answer in seconds, and because it knows the range, it points them to the right potting mix and feed to match. Staff got their floor time back, and the average sale crept up.

Take from it: pick the question your team answers fifty times a day and let AI take the first pass. You’re not replacing your people. You’re freeing them for the part that makes the sale.

Prompt of the Week

The three messages that get an overdue invoice paid.

Chasing money is the job everyone hates and everyone puts off, which is exactly why it’s worth handing to AI. This writes you a calm, escalating set of three reminders so you never have to draft the awkward one from scratch again.

You write the kind of payment reminders that get an invoice paid without damaging the relationship. I run a small commercial cleaning business. A regular client, Harbourside Dental, hasn’t paid invoice 1043 for $880, now 21 days overdue. They’ve always paid eventually, just slowly, and I want to keep them. Write me three short reminder messages I can send by email a week apart. The first is warm and assumes they simply missed it. The second is friendly but clearer, restates the invoice number and amount, and asks for a payment date. The third is polite but firm, names a final date, and mentions I’ll pause the next clean until it’s settled, without sounding aggressive. Keep them plain and human, the way an Australian small business owner actually writes, no corporate stiffness and no threats, under ninety words each.

Use it for: Swap in your own client, invoice number and amount, and you’ve got a full chase sequence ready to send. Most owners get the first one out before their coffee’s cold.

The Magnum

The 3D Fridge Magnet Collection.

When you need highly specific, styled merchandise or product concepts but don’t want to pay a 3D artist just to see if the idea works. This prompt forces a clean, top-down flat-lay that’s perfect for pitching merchandise ideas. Run it in ChatGPT and swap in your own show or movie.

ChatGPT 2.0 Image: “A flat-lay collection of exactly 9 hyper-realistic 3D resin fridge magnets arranged on a bright pure white surface in a clean 3x3 grid, themed around [SHOW/MOVIE]. At the very top center, a wide rectangular text magnet spells ‘[SHOW/MOVIE]’ in bold 3D block letters styled in the show’s exact signature typography and colors. Below it, 9 magnets in a clean 3x3 grid: the first row contains 3 chibi full-body character figurines of the most iconic characters, perfectly detailed and instantly recognizable. The second and third rows contain 6 of the most iconic props, locations, and symbols from the show — objects any casual viewer would immediately recognize. Each magnet is small, neatly spaced with generous white space between them, slightly raised with a soft drop shadow beneath. Bright white background, warm studio lighting from above, photorealistic product render, top-down flat-lay angle, 2:3 aspect ratio.”

The power here isn’t just generating an image; it’s enforcing a rigid layout structure (the 3x3 grid) so the AI acts like a product photographer, not just an illustrator.

Before you shut this, copy the invoice prompt above, swap in one client who owes you, and send the first reminder. Five minutes, and you might be paid by Friday.
